A note on word-final sounds

At the end of a word, voiced consonants are pronounced as their voiceless partners — the same as in Bulgarian:

  • thud "milk" is said thut
  • jag "fire" is said jak
  • diz "city" is said dis
  • thuv "smoke" is said thuf

The voicing comes back as soon as a vowel-initial ending follows: jagà "fires", dizà "cities".
