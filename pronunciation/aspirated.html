Aspirated consonants

Erli has four consonants that come with a strong puff of breath right after them. They are written as digraphs ending in h, with one exception, čš. The puff is meaningful and can change the word.

LetterHow it soundsMinimal pair
khlike k, with extra breathkher (house) vs ker! (make!)
phlike p, with extra breath. Not like English ph in phone, which is just /f/.pheràv (I fill) vs peràv (I fall)
thlike t, with extra breath. Not like English th in this or thin.thud (milk) vs tut (you, accusative)
čšlike č (English ch) followed by a clear š. Church plus sh run together.čšurì (knife) vs čurì (braid)

In other Romani varieties the same sound as čš is usually written čh. The Sofia Erli spelling reflects that here the aspiration is pronounced as an audible š instead of just a puff.
